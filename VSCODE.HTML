Ratan Tata

A Visionary Leader, Industrialist & Philanthropist

Biography

Born: December 28, 1937

Education: Cornell University (Architecture & Structural Engineering), Harvard Business School (Advanced Management Program)

Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist and the former chairman of Tata Sons. Under his leadership, Tata Group expanded globally, acquiring major brands like Jaguar Land Rover and Tetley.

Career & Achievements

  • Joined Tata Group in 1961, working on the Tata Steel shop floor.
  • Became chairman of Tata Sons in 1991, leading a massive transformation of the group.
  • Led acquisitions of Tetley Tea, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus Steel.
  • Launched the Tata Nano, the world's most affordable car, making transportation accessible to millions.
  • Expanded Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) into a global IT powerhouse.

Major Contributions

1. Tata Nano: Revolutionized the automobile industry by launching the most affordable car.

2. Global Expansion: Acquired international brands like JLR and Tetley, making Tata a global name.

3. Technology & Innovation: Strengthened India's IT sector through TCS, India's leading software company.

4. Space & Defence: Tata Advanced Systems contributed to India's space missions.
